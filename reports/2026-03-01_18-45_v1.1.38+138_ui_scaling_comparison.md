## 1. Visual Comparison Overview

The two themes follow radically different philosophies for metadata density. While the "Fruit" theme is physically shorter (reduced vertical space), its internal elements (stars and badges) are often larger or more styled than their "Android" counterparts.

## 2. Technical Findings

### A. The "Badge" Paradox
One of the most striking differences is that the **Badge** (showing SHNID count or Source ID) actually becomes **significantly larger** in the Fruit theme, even though the card itself is shorter.
- **Android logic:** `(7.0) * effectiveScale`
- **Fruit logic:** `(9.5) * effectiveScale`
- **Result:** In the Fruit theme, the badge often feels like a primary UI anchor, whereas in Android, it is a secondary "fine print" element.

### B. Star Placement and Scaling
The "Star" (RatingControl) uses a fixed base size of `19` or `20`, but the internal rendering varies:
- **Android:** Uses a standard `RatingBar` with `Icons.star`. It feels larger because the Material star is "fuller" in its vector path.
- **Fruit:** Uses `LucideIcons.star` which has a thinner stroke. 
- **Fruit Neumorphism:** When `useNeumorphism` is active, the Fruit theme applies an additional `0.95x` scale multiplier to the `itemSize` and wraps it in a `NeumorphicWrapper`. This creates a "sunken" effect (a basin) that makes the star appear physically smaller and deeper in the UI compared to the "flat" Android star.

### C. Layout Positioning
Both themes use identical positioning logic:
- `Positioned(right: 12.0 * style.effectiveScale)`
- However, since Fruit cards are `34px` high and Android are `40px` high, the stars and badges in Fruit are much closer to the top/bottom boundaries of the card, contributing to the "Liquid Glass" edge-to-edge aesthetic.

## 3. Summary Conclusion
The **Android theme** prioritizes **legibility through whitespace** (larger card, smaller text).  
The **Fruit theme** prioritizes **visual "pop"** (larger badges, styled icons, thinner rows). 

The Fruit badge's `9.5` font size compared to Android's `7.0` is the single biggest contributor to the perceived difference in "stacked" density.
