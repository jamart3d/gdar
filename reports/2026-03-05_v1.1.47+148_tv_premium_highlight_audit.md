# TV Premium Highlight Audit Report

**Date**: 2026-03-05
**Version**: 1.1.47+148
**Feature**: Premium TV Highlight (Settings > Interface)

## 1. Executive Summary
The "Premium TV Highlight" feature in version `1.1.47+148` provides a high-fidelity animated gradient border for TV focus states. While visually impressive, the current implementation in `TvFocusWrapper` and `AnimatedGradientBorder` presents a layout overlap issue and potential performance overhead on lower-end TV hardware. The core UI flow and navigation logic remain unchanged and fully functional.

---

## 2. Technical Findings

### 🔴 Layout Issue: Border-Content Overlap
*   **Observation**: `TvFocusWrapper` implements the premium highlight using `AnimatedGradientBorder` with `usePadding: false` and a `borderWidth` of `6.0`.
*   **Root Cause**: In `AnimatedGradientBorder`, the stroke is centered on the widget boundary (`rect.deflate(borderWidth / 2)`). With `6.0` width, this results in a `3.0px` overlap into the child's content area.
*   **Impact**: UI elements like icons or text that sit close to the edge of a `TvListTile` or `TvSwitchListTile` are partially obscured or clipped by the neon gradient.
*   **Recommendation**: Set `usePadding: true` in `TvFocusWrapper` for premium states, or increase the internal margins of the child widgets.

### 🟡 Performance: Constant Repaint
*   **Observation**: The highlight utilizes an `AnimatedBuilder` that triggers a `CustomPaint` repaint every frame while the item is focused.
*   **Context**: `TvFocusWrapper` is used for almost every interactive element on TV (28+ files).
*   **Impact**: While only one item is focused at a time, the constant animation loop remains active. On certain TV SOCs, this can lead to increased GPU load and potential "jank" when scrolling quickly.
*   **Recommendation**: Consider using a `RepaintBoundary` around the `AnimatedGradientBorder` or lowering the frame rate of the glow animation on TV devices.

### 🟢 Flow Verification: Navigation & Interaction
*   **Verification**: Navigation flow (D-Pad) was analyzed by inspecting the `Focus` node implementation in `TvFocusWrapper`.
*   **Result**: The feature is a visual decorator only. The `Focus` node and `GestureDetector` (Tap/Long Press) are located outside the `AnimatedGradientBorder` wrapper.
*   **Conclusion**: Enabling the highlight does not change focus traversal, selection logic, or general UI flow.

## 5. Follow-up Audit: Swapped Highlights & Flow Issues (v1.1.48)

### 2. Swapped / Invisible Highlight Logic in TVs Show List
**Diagnosis:** 
The user reported that the active playing show border on the left panel was either swapped to the premium Neon color or entirely missing/invisible. Two causes were identified:
1.  **Prioritization Bug:** `TvFocusWrapper` evaluated `showPremium` before `showPlayingRgb`. If a playing item was focused while premium was on, it incorrectly displayed the premium layout.
2.  **Structural Padding Bug:** `ShowListItem` wraps its card in a `Padding` layout with 16px of horizontal inset. `TvFocusWrapper` was placed *outside* this padding. Consequently, when `TvFocusWrapper` attempted to draw the playing RGB border, the border was drawn 16px away from the actual card edge, making it appear invisible or floating incorrectly.

**Fix:**
- Reordered `TvFocusWrapper.build` conditions so `showPlayingRgb` takes precedence over `showPremium`.
- Restored standard Rainbow Colors to `TvFocusWrapper`'s playing state.
- **Crucial Layout Fix:** Removed `isPlaying` from `ShowListItem`'s call to `TvFocusWrapper`, entirely preventing it from drawing the detached boundary. Simultaneously reverted a recent `!isTv` restriction inside `ShowListCard` itself, restoring its ability to correctly draw the tight, un-padded Rainbow border around playing shows on TV.

### 🔴 Logic Issue: Premium Highlight Swapped with Playing State
*   **Observation**: In the Show List (left panel), the currently playing item would display the Neon Premium border instead of the distinct Rainbow Playing border, leading to a perceived "swapped" state.
*   **Root Cause**: In `TvFocusWrapper`, the `_isFocused && sp.oilTvPremiumHighlight` condition was evaluated *before* the `showPlayingRgb` condition. Therefore, if the playing item was focused, it incorrectly received the standard focus border instead of its dedicated playing border. Additionally, the non-glow variant of the playing border was missing its specific `colors` array.
*   **Resolution**: 
    1. Reordered the conditions in `TvFocusWrapper.build` so `showPlayingRgb` takes precedence.
    2. Restored the explicit `colors` array (Rainbow) for the playing state, keeping Neon Pink/Cyan/Blue for the standard premium focus.

### 🔴 Flow Issue: Show List to Track List Navigation
*   **Observation**: Users reported difficulty navigating from the left panel (Show List) to the right panel (Track List) using the D-Pad.
*   **Root Cause**: The horizontal handoff was not explicitly managed. Pressing 'Right' on a focused `ShowListCard` would get trapped in the left pane's `ListView` focus scope or jump to unintended header elements.
*   **Resolution**: Added an explicit `onFocusRight` callback chain from `ShowListItem` up through `ShowListBody` to `TvDualPaneLayout`. When triggered by `LogicalKeyboardKey.arrowRight`, it now explicitly requests focus on the `_rightPaneFocusNode` (containing the Track List), ensuring a reliable and intentional leap between UI zones.

### 🔴 Rendering Issue: Highlight "Still On After Toggle Off"
*   **Observation**: The standard RGB border was sometimes invisible, or the neon border appeared to persist incorrectly when toggling settings.
*   **Root Cause**: The `showGlow` property in `AnimatedGradientBorder` was acting as a master render switch instead of just controlling the shadow.
*   **Resolution**: Decoupled the border rendering from the glow effect by ensuring `showGlow: true` is passed even for non-glow borders, but coupled it with `showShadow: false` to achieve the intended crisp RGB line.
